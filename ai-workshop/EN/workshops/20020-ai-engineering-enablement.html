20020 · AI-assisted Engineering 06/2026

Practical workshop for technical teams in mid-sized companies.

Using AI in daily engineering work in a meaningful, explainable and responsible way.

Enablement

Core idea

This workshop is not AI marketing. It is not about buzzwords, frameworks or prompt hacks. It focuses on real engineering problems: how can AI help with actual technical work?

  • Reality instead of hype: capabilities, limits and hallucinations
  • Increase productivity in everyday engineering work
  • Structure requirements, challenge assumptions and detect gaps
  • Document architectures, interfaces and decisions
  • Prepare security reviews and support security-by-design
  • Evaluate cloud services, local models and RAG systems

Audience

Engineering managers, architects, embedded developers, security leads, project managers and senior engineers.

Format

4 hours, remote or on-site, interactive, around 6 to 20 participants.

Central message

AI does not replace engineers, architects or critical thinking. But AI can accelerate and support many activities. Teams that learn to use AI as a tool become more productive. Teams that trust AI blindly create errors.

Workshop contents

Engineering work

  • Accelerate research
  • Create documentation
  • Analyze specifications
  • Prepare reviews and meeting notes
  • Improve knowledge management

Requirements & architecture

  • Structure requirements
  • Identify unclear wording
  • Compare alternatives
  • Document decisions
  • Visualize technical relationships

Security & compliance

  • Identify threats
  • Review code and architectures
  • Protect company knowledge
  • Address privacy and IP protection
  • Understand limits of automated assessments

Tools

  • ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude
  • GitHub Copilot
  • Mammut.io
  • OpenWebUI and local models
  • RAG systems

What makes it different

The workshop is delivered by an experienced technical architect with 25 years of engineering experience across software architecture, system architecture, embedded systems, security engineering, automotive environments and technical leadership roles. Real technical problems are the center of the workshop.

Possible follow-up formats

Architecture review, security review, AI sparring, introduction of local models, analysis of engineering workflows or technical consulting. No aggressive upselling and no artificial consulting machinery.
