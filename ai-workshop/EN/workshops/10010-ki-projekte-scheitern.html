10010 · AI Readiness

Why 80% of AI projects fail before the first prompt.

Why will your AI initiative probably fail within six months? Because technology is rarely the first problem.

Reality Check

Core idea

Today's AI technologies will be replaced quickly. What matters is whether an organization can integrate AI into its work in a controlled, useful and sustainable way.

  • Understand AI readiness realistically
  • Structure data, knowledge and documentation
  • Introduce local and cloud AI with clear boundaries
  • Clarify governance, privacy and accountability early
  • Create practical value in everyday work

Typical risk

A pilot works, but the organization learns nothing from it.

Outcome

A clear view of prerequisites, blockers and meaningful next steps.

Which problem does this workshop solve?

Many AI initiatives start with tools before the organization has clarified what AI should support, which data may be used and who remains accountable. This workshop creates a sober starting point before budget, time and trust are invested in isolated pilots.

Who is it for?

For executive teams, CTOs, engineering managers, system architects and technical decision makers who want to understand whether their organization is ready for AI and where the real blockers are.

What do participants take away?

  • A realistic view of why AI projects fail early
  • A shared understanding of organizational prerequisites
  • Clarity about data, knowledge, privacy and responsibility gaps
  • A first decision basis for meaningful next steps

Why is ChatGPT alone not enough?

ChatGPT can answer questions, but it does not fix missing knowledge structures, unclear ownership, poor documentation or unmanaged risks. AI only becomes useful when it is embedded in engineering work, decision paths and review culture.

How does the workshop run?

We examine existing AI ideas, engineering workflows, knowledge sources, data quality, privacy boundaries and expected business value. The workshop separates useful AI adoption from tool-driven experimentation.

Which prerequisites are needed?

No AI implementation is required. Helpful inputs are current questions, known pain points, existing use case ideas or examples of documentation and knowledge work that currently slow teams down.

What result is available at the end?

The organization receives a clear view of readiness, risks, missing prerequisites and practical next steps. The result is not a tool choice, but a grounded starting point for responsible AI adoption.

Which workshop fits afterwards?

Depending on maturity, the next step is usually AI Strategy & Readiness or Secure AI Engineering.
