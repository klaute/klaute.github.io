50050 · AI Governance & Responsible AI

Using AI responsibly and in line with regulations.

Practical guardrails for privacy, information security, risk decisions and secure enterprise use.

Governance

Topics

  • Understand GDPR and the EU AI Act
  • Address privacy and information security
  • Understand risks and limits of generative AI
  • Decide when AI is useful and when it should not be used
  • Develop practical guidelines for secure enterprise adoption

Typical risk

Rules are created only after teams already use critical data in production workflows.

Outcome

Clear and practical guardrails for teams and decision makers.

Which problem does this workshop solve?

Teams often start using AI before responsibilities, data boundaries and risk decisions are clear. This workshop turns governance into practical rules that technical teams can actually apply.

Who is it for?

For CTOs, engineering managers, security leads, architects and responsible decision makers who need clear guardrails for AI use in engineering, documentation and knowledge work.

What do participants take away?

  • A practical view of GDPR, the EU AI Act and information security risks
  • Criteria for when AI may be used and when it should not be used
  • Guidelines for handling source code, documents and company knowledge
  • A foundation for secure and responsible team rules

Why is ChatGPT alone not enough?

The risk is not only the model response. The risk is also what teams enter, which decisions they delegate, how results are reviewed and who remains responsible. Governance must be connected to real workflows.

How does the workshop run?

We review typical AI use cases, data categories, risk areas, legal and security constraints, review duties and decision points where AI should be limited or deliberately avoided.

Which prerequisites are needed?

Useful preparation includes existing AI usage, internal guidelines, security requirements, privacy concerns and examples of sensitive data or engineering workflows.

What result is available at the end?

The organization receives concrete governance principles, decision criteria and guardrails that can be translated into internal rules, onboarding and review processes.

Which workshop fits afterwards?

If technical architecture is still unclear, continue with Secure AI Engineering. If teams should apply the rules in practice, continue with AI-assisted Engineering.
