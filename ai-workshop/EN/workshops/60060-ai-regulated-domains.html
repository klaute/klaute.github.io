60060 · AI for Regulated Domains

Using AI where it supports people without replacing responsibility.

Structured information processing for regulated, documentation-heavy and responsibility-intensive environments.

Regulated

Topics

  • Improve documentation and knowledge preparation
  • Support administration and operational processes
  • Assist with regulatory requirements
  • Structure and search large document collections
  • Relieve experts through intelligent information processing

Focus

AI as assistance for knowledge, structure and preparation, not as a replacement for accountability.

Suitable for

Organizations with high documentation load, compliance requirements and scarce expert capacity.

Which problem does this workshop solve?

Regulated environments often lose time in documentation, search, preparation and administrative knowledge work. This workshop shows where AI can relieve experts without delegating accountability to a model.

Who is it for?

For technical leaders, architects, quality and compliance stakeholders in regulated, documentation-heavy or responsibility-intensive domains such as medtech, automotive, industry and embedded engineering.

What do participants take away?

  • Realistic AI use cases for documentation and knowledge preparation
  • An understanding of where AI can assist without replacing review
  • Approaches for structuring large document collections
  • Guidance for privacy, traceability and human responsibility

Why is ChatGPT alone not enough?

A chat response is not a controlled process. Regulated domains need traceable inputs, structured knowledge, reviewable outputs, clear responsibility and boundaries for what AI may support.

How does the workshop run?

We identify documentation-heavy workflows, recurring information needs, regulatory support tasks, data boundaries and review points. The focus is on assistance, preparation and structure, not on automated decision making.

Which prerequisites are needed?

Helpful inputs are examples of recurring documents, knowledge sources, compliance tasks, administrative workflows and areas where experts spend time preparing or searching information.

What result is available at the end?

Participants leave with a practical view of suitable AI support scenarios, required safeguards and next steps for reducing knowledge and documentation effort responsibly.

Which workshop fits afterwards?

For technical implementation, continue with Secure AI Engineering. For organizational guardrails, continue with AI Governance & Responsible AI.
