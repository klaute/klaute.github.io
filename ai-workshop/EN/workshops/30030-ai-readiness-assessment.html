30030 · AI Strategy & Readiness

Preparing organizations for meaningful AI decisions.

A pragmatic look at models, cloud versus local deployment, privacy, infrastructure, use cases and real adoption blockers.

Strategy

Topics

  • Evaluate suitable models and operating models
  • Assess cloud versus local systems
  • Clarify privacy, confidentiality and infrastructure
  • Prioritize relevant use cases
  • Assess data quality and documentation maturity
  • Identify technical and organizational blockers
  • Develop a pragmatic AI roadmap

Typical risk

AI is introduced before knowledge, data and responsibilities are ready.

Outcome

A reliable basis for decisions, investments and next steps.

Which problem does this workshop solve?

Organizations often have AI ideas, tool proposals and pressure to act, but no reliable view of prerequisites, risks or priorities. This workshop turns scattered assumptions into a pragmatic decision basis.

Who is it for?

For CTOs, engineering managers, architects and technical leadership teams that need to decide where AI should start, which use cases are realistic and which conditions must be created first.

What do participants take away?

  • A structured view of relevant AI use cases
  • An assessment of data, documentation and knowledge maturity
  • Clarity on cloud, local models, privacy and infrastructure questions
  • A pragmatic roadmap instead of isolated pilot activity

Why is ChatGPT alone not enough?

A chat tool does not answer strategic questions about operating models, data boundaries, cost, responsibility or integration into engineering processes. These decisions require architecture, security and operational thinking.

How does the workshop run?

We analyze existing workflows, knowledge sources, candidate use cases, privacy constraints, infrastructure options and organizational blockers. The focus is on realistic adoption paths rather than generic AI enthusiasm.

Which prerequisites are needed?

No technical AI setup is required. Useful preparation includes current AI ideas, known process pain points, documentation examples and questions around cloud, local deployment or data protection.

What result is available at the end?

Participants leave with a prioritized view of where AI can create value, which prerequisites are missing and which next steps are technically and organizationally sensible.

Which workshop fits afterwards?

If the next step is implementation, continue with Secure AI Engineering. If teams need practical enablement, continue with AI-assisted Engineering.
